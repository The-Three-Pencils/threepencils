About The Tempest

'The Tempest' painting

The Tempest is a play made by William Shakespeare, written in 1610 to 1611. It was thought to be Shakespeare’s last play written.

The Superstitious Era – King James I

Most of Shakespeare’s plays were written during the Elizabethan Era, which happened between 17 November 1558 to 24 March 1603. King James was the successor the Queen Elizabeth. He was extremely superstitious and was afraid of witchcraft. He was so afraid that he passed a law in the Government which banned witchery, which ended in 1951.

The Power of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

Witchcraft and Wizardry

As the technology we have can see why natural disasters occur, people during King James’ era made up different reasons why they happened. The most common reason said was witchery. Because of these accusations, women were blamed for witchery. Women and cats were beaten or tortured. It was thought that witches could see into the future, cast spells, cause bad weather and disguise themselves as creatures such as cats.

Around the World

An oil painting of Sir Francis Drake.

Ordinary items like potatoes and tomatoes didn’t originate in the UK. It was actually introduced to the UK in the mid-16th century, when Sir Walter Raleigh and Sir Francis Drake travelled around the world using ships. They went to places like America to start new colonies. The natives had “exotic” items – things not found in the UK like tobacco, tomatoes, potatoes. At the time when these were introduced in the UK, only the rich could be able to afford it and they were only used for display, not for eating. Tomatoes were massively popular because of its red colour.

Sir Francis Drake is a man who rose to be one of the richest men in Devon. He set out with 5 ships with 200 men. 3 years later, having sailed around the world, he returned with just 1 ship and 56 men, but with a lot of treasure. He stole a lot of loot from the Spanish, valued at around 600,000 pounds which, even at this time, is a big fortune. For this success to be possible, it was the gunpowder and cannons that had a part in this. The Golden Hind, the ship used to sail around the world, was armed with 18 cannons – 14 of these are capable of shooting an iron cannonball, weighing roughly 4 lbs into his enemy’s ships.
